History of Ttekbokki

Ttekbokki originated in Korea, and its history can be traced back to the Joseon Dynasty (1392‚Äì1897). Initially, it was made with plain rice cakes in a mild sauce. Over time, it evolved into the spicy and flavorful version we know today. The addition of gochujang (Korean red chili paste) in the 1950s transformed it into the popular street food enjoyed across South Korea. Originally a royal dish, it was later adapted by the public and became a common comfort food, especially in markets and street vendors.

Ingredients of Ttekbokki
  • Tteok (Rice cakes): Soft, chewy cylindrical rice cakes, also called "garaetteok."
  • Gochujang (Korean red chili paste): The key ingredient for the spicy sauce.
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): Optional, for added spice.
  • Soy sauce: Adds umami flavor.
  • Sugar: For sweetness to balance the spiciness.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic for aroma.
  • Sesame oil: For depth of flavor.
  • Water: To help create the sauce.
  • Vegetables: Often onions, cabbage, or scallions
  • Boiled eggs (optional): Sometimes added as a garnish.
  • Fish cakes (optional): A common addition in traditional tteokbokki recipes.
    • How to Cook tteokbokki
    1. Prepare the rice cakes: If using fresh or frozen rice cakes, soak them in water for about 20-30 minutes to soften. If using pre-cooked rice cakes, they can be used directly.
    2. Make the sauce: In a saucepan, combine about 2 tablespoons of gochujang, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1-2 tablespoons of sugar, 1 clove of minced garlic, and 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Add about 1 cup of water (adjust for desired sauce consistency) and bring to a simmer. Stir to dissolve the gochujang and mix the ingredients.
    3. Cook the rice cakes: Add the soaked rice cakes to the simmering sauce and cook for about 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice cakes become soft and chewy and the sauce thickens.
    4. Add fish cakes and vegetables: If using, add fish cakes and any vegetables like onions, cabbage, or scallions during the last few minutes of cooking.
    5. Garnish: Top with a boiled egg, sesame seeds, or chopped green onions before serving.
    Additional Info
      Variations:
  • Cheese tteokbokki: A modern variation where melted cheese is added to the dish, giving it a creamy texture.
  • Spicy tteokbokki: Additional chili flakes or hot peppers can be added for extra heat.
  • Yukhoe tteokbokki: Tteokbokki served with raw beef, often as a more luxurious version.
